## 1. GUARANTEE — Offensive Tool, Not Safety Net

### Core Principle (Hormozi)
Risk inversion transfers the burden FROM the buyer TO the seller.
A strong guarantee ATTRACTS serious buyers and FILTERS skeptics.

**Counter-intuitive result:** Aggressive guarantee → -8 to -15% refunds (not +).
Why: Buyers who feel safe → buy without hesitation → implement immediately →
get results → no reason to refund.

---

### The 4 Guarantee Types (ranked by conversion impact)

#### TYPE 1 — BETTER-THAN-MONEY-BACK ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Description:** Customer is refunded full purchase price PLUS receives additional compensation.
**Impact:** Up to +52% conversion on some products
**When to use:** High-sophistication markets (level 4-5 from skill 09), very bold promise

**Copy template:**
```
"If you don't [Core Promise] within [N] days of applying this system,
I'll refund every penny you paid — AND I'll also [Additional Compensation:
send you $X credit / give you 3 months free / include a bonus session].

You literally cannot lose."
```

**Examples of "extra" compensation:**
- $50 credit toward next purchase
- Free 30-min strategy session ($197 value)
- 3 additional months of community access
- Full refund + keep all downloads + bonus credit

---

#### TYPE 2 — KEEP-THE-PRODUCT ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Description:** Customer receives full refund AND keeps all digital assets downloaded.
**Impact:** "Zero risk" perception — ideal for micro-offer digitals ($17-$47)
**When to use:** First-purchase offers, cold traffic, TOFU/MOFU audiences
**Why it works:** If they keep it → they're invested → they use it → they don't refund.

**Copy template:**
```
"Try [Product Name] for [N] days. If it's not exactly what I described,
email [address] and I'll refund every dollar — no questions asked.

Keep every template, every bonus, and every resource you downloaded.
Consider them my thank-you for giving it a shot."
```

**Key phrase:** "no questions asked" — removes friction of justification.
Never make buyer write an essay to get a refund.

---

#### TYPE 3 — CONDITIONAL ⭐⭐⭐
**Description:** Refund issued if buyer demonstrates they implemented the system.
**Impact:** +73% course completion rate. Filters serial refunders.
**When to use:** Implementation-heavy products, accountability-driven personas
**Prerequisite from skill 09:** Persona must be "effort-willing" (not "quick-fix seeking")

**Copy template:**
```
"If you complete [Specific Actions: the 7 daily lessons / all 5 templates / the 30-day plan]
and don't see [Specific Result] within [N] days, show me your work and I'll
refund your entire investment, no questions.

If you put in the work and it doesn't deliver — I don't deserve your money."
```

**Conditions must be:**
- Specific and objectively verifiable (screenshots, submissions)
- Reasonable (not "finish 200 hours of content")
- Tied to the core promise timeline

---

#### TYPE 4 — IMPLEMENTATION GUARANTEE ⭐⭐
**Description:** Seller accompanies buyer until they reach the result.
**When to use:** Premium price tier ($97+) — too labor-intensive for $17-$47
**Note:** Not recommended for this skill's price band. Document for reference only.

### DURATION — The 60-Day Sweet Spot

| Duration | Conversion vs 30 days | Refund rate change |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| 14 days | -18% | — |
| 30 days | baseline | baseline |
| 60 days | +23% | neutral (no increase) |
| 90 days | +31% | +4% refunds |
| Lifetime | +41% | +12% refunds |

**Recommendation for $17-$47:** 60 days.
- Long enough to feel safe → higher conversion
- Not so long that buyer forgets → refund window doesn't compound
- 90 days and lifetime start showing meaningful refund rate increases

**Psychology behind 60 days:**
The longer window REDUCES urgency to test immediately.
Buyer relaxes → implements → gets results → window passes naturally → no refund.
Compared to 30 days: buyer panics → tests hastily → doesn't implement properly → refunds.

### GUARANTEE PLACEMENT RULES

**Primary placement:** Block 9 (Risk Reversal block) — full copy + duration + conditions
**Secondary placement (condensed):** Micro-copy under every CTA button
**Checkout placement:** Brief reminder adjacent to card fields (reduces abandonment)

**Guarantee seal design:**
- Round badge with "GARANTIE [N] JOURS" text
- Checkmark or shield icon (trust symbol)
- Placed within 2.5cm of Buy CTA (Proximity Rule — same as social proof)

## 2. SCARCITY & URGENCY — Authentic Mechanics Only

### Absolute Rule — Zero Fake Scarcity
FTC enforcement in 2026: fake countdown timers and invented limits are sanctionable.
Beyond legal risk: modern buyers detect fake urgency instantly → trust destruction.
"Crying wolf" syndrome: fake urgency on launch → skepticism on all future launches.

**The authenticity test:**
"If the urgency mechanism ceased to exist tomorrow due to external factors,
would the product still be for sale at the same price with the same content?"
If YES → it's real scarcity. If NO → it's fake.

---

### MECHANISM 1 — PHASED BONUS DEPLETION (Default for $17-$47 digital)

**How it works:**
- Core product: always available, price never changes
- Bonuses: removed sequentially on a schedule
- Creates real diminishing value without fake "only 3 PDFs left" claims

**Standard depletion schedule:**

```
Day 0 Launch:
└── Full Stack available:
    ├── Core Offer
    ├── Bonus 1 (Plug-and-Play)
    ├── Bonus 2 (Speed/Shortcuts)
    ├── Fast-Action Bonus
    └── Guarantee (permanent)

Days 1-2 (Launch Window):
└── Full Stack — urgency: "Fast-Action Bonus expires in [N] hours"
    Copy: "This complete pack — including [Fast-Action Bonus Name] — is only guaranteed
    until [Deadline]. After that, [Bonus Name] is gone. Everything else remains."

Days 3-4 (Post Fast-Action):
├── Fast-Action Bonus: ❌ EXPIRED
├── Available: Core + Bonus 1 + Bonus 2 + Guarantee
└── Copy: "[Fast-Action Bonus Name] has closed. But you can still get everything else,
    including [Bonus 1] and [Bonus 2]. [Bonus 2] expires [Date]."

Day 5+ (Evergreen minimum):
├── Bonus 2: ❌ EXPIRED
├── Available: Core + Bonus 1 + Guarantee
└── Copy: "The core [Product Name] and [Bonus 1] are still available.
    [Bonus 2] is no longer included. Get what's left while the full deal stands."
```

**Tech stack for implementation:**
- Deadline Funnel: cookies + IP address + email tracking (three-layer tracking)
- Ensures countdown timer persists across devices and sessions
- NOT: page-refresh timer (resets on every page load — buyers catch this)

---

### MECHANISM 2 — CAPACITY SCARCITY (Services/Accountability components)

**When to use:** When offer includes a human-touch element (live Q&A, group coaching, audit)
**Why it's authentic:** Seller genuinely cannot serve unlimited buyers simultaneously

**Copy template:**
```
"To maintain the quality of [Live Q&A / Group Coaching / Audit Sessions],
we cap enrollment at [N] new members per [week/month/cohort].

[N] spots remain for this [week/month/cohort].
After that, you join the waitlist."
```

**Constraints:**
- Number must be real (enforce it operationally)
- Update the number as spots fill
- Show social proof: "[N] enrolled this [week] already"

---

### MECHANISM 3 — COMBINED SCARCITY + SOCIAL PROOF

**Formula:** Depleting quantity + buying momentum in one statement.

```
"847 people bought this in the last 30 days.
The [Fast-Action Bonus Name] expires in [Time].
Once it's gone, it's gone — and I don't know when (or if) I'll bring it back."
```

**Dual bias activation:**
1. Scarcity → loss aversion ("what if I miss out?")
2. Social proof → conformity ("847 people can't all be wrong")

**Placement:** Above hero CTA, below testimonials grid, in email subject lines

## 3. AOV STACK ARCHITECTURE

### ORDER BUMP — $12-$19 (Pre-checkout)

**Psychology:** Buyer has already made the primary purchase decision.
Adding a small amount at checkout activates:
- Yes Momentum (commitment/consistency) — they're already saying yes
- Effort heuristic — adding more feels like completing, not spending
- Loss framing: "Add [X] today before you lose access to this checkout price"

**Target take rate:** 37.8% (industry benchmark for well-matched order bumps)

**Matching rule:** Order bump must be a logical EXTENSION of the core offer,
not a separate product. The buyer should think "obviously I need this too."

**Examples by core offer:**
| Core Offer | Logical Order Bump |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Fitness guide ($47) | Printable 21-day tracking journal ($17) |
| Productivity system ($27) | Notion template pack extension ($14) |
| Finance starter ($37) | Monthly budget spreadsheet pack ($12) |
| Content creator guide ($47) | 50 plug-and-play caption templates ($19) |

**Checkbox format (critical):**
```
☐ YES — Add [Bump Name] to my order for just $[Price]
   → [Specific immediate benefit today]
   → Normally $[Higher Price] — today only at checkout
```

**Dynamic Order Bump (2026 differentiator):**
Vary the bump offer based on:
- Referral source (Meta → visual-heavy offer; TikTok → quick/viral offer)
- Quiz responses (collected pre-checkout)
- Cart contents (complement, not duplicate)
Tech: Carthook, ThriveCart, or custom JS conditional logic.

---

### OTO — ONE TIME OFFER, $51-$100 (Thank You page)

**Placement:** Immediately after purchase confirmation (Thank You page).
**Timing:** Buyer is in "buying spree" state — dopamine peak, commitment activated.

**Mechanics for maximum conversion:**
- One-click add (no re-entering card details)
- 15-minute countdown timer (authentic — "this offer disappears when you leave this page")
- Video explanation recommended (60-90 seconds)

**Target acceptance rate:** 15-25% (higher = extremely well-matched OTO)

**Opening formula (non-negotiable):**
```
"Before you close this page — your order is confirmed and on its way.
But I noticed something about what you just bought, and I want to offer you
something that makes [Core Offer] 3x more powerful."
```

**Bundling rule (Hormozi):**
For recurring offers: bundle the setup fee + first month in ONE transaction.
Never present as two separate charges (breaks commitment momentum, reduces acceptance 40%).

**OTO structure:**
1. Opening: "Before you close this page…"
2. Bridge: "Here's why this [OTO] pairs perfectly with [Core Offer]…"
3. Offer reveal: What it is, what it does, what life looks like with both
4. Proof: 1-2 testimonials from people who got both
5. Price reveal + contrast: "Usually $[Higher]. Today, one-click, $[OTO Price]."
6. CTA: "Yes, add [OTO] to my order →" / "No thanks, I'll figure it out myself"

---

### DOWNSELL (If OTO rejected)

**Trigger:** Buyer clicks "No thanks" on OTO.
**Rule:** NEVER show a price discount on the same product (destroys OTO's value perception).
Instead: different version OR payment plan.

**Option A — Lite Version:**
```
"No problem. Here's a lighter version of [OTO Name] at $[Lower Price] —
covers [Subset of OTO benefits] without [Premium element].
One click to add it to your order."
```

**Option B — Payment Plan:**
```
"Not the right time for $[OTO Price]? I get it.
Start with [Month 1 payment] today, and [Month 2 payment] in 30 days.
Same everything — just split across two months."
```

**Selection guide:**
- TOFU/MOFU personas → Payment Plan (lower commitment threshold)
- BOFU personas → Lite Version (they know what they want, just not sure about price)

## 4. PAIN OF PAYING — Checkout Friction Reduction

Anchored from consumer behavior research (credit card fee studies, checkout abandonment data).

### Friction points and solutions

| Friction Point | Solution | Impact |
|---------------|----------|--------|
| Credit card form feels risky | SSL badge + "Secured by [Provider]" adjacent to fields | -18% abandonment |
| Seeing "$" symbol | Remove $ from sales page copy (keep on checkout form) | -4% purchase anxiety |
| Account creation required | Guest checkout default (account created post-purchase) | -24% abandonment |
| Price feels large | BNPL: Klarna/Afterpay for $37+ products | +30-50% AOV |
| Card details anxiety | Apple Pay / Google Pay → biometric 2-click | -31% mobile abandonment |
| Uncertainty about product | Guarantee reminder at checkout ("Still covered by 60-day guarantee") | -14% cart abandonment |

### BNPL threshold rule:
- $17-$36: BNPL optional (minimal impact)
- $37-$47: BNPL recommended (+30% AOV minimum)
- Providers: Klarna (Europe), Afterpay (US/Aus), Affirm (US)

### Trust badge hierarchy (place in order):
1. SSL Certificate badge (security — always first)
2. Payment processor logo (Stripe, PayPal)
3. Guarantee badge (keep-product / money-back seal)
4. "Secure Checkout" text + lock icon
5. Media mentions (if applicable)
