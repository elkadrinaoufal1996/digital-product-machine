# Persona Framework — Skill 04

Reference for building empirical buyer personas in Skill 04.
Unique to this skill. No scoring matrices, no hooks, no offer structure here.

---

## Consciousness Level Framework (Eugene Schwartz — Adapted for Digital Products)

Use this to assign the correct awareness level to each persona based on how they write and speak.

### L1 — Unaware
Buyer doesn't know their problem has a name or a solution category.
Signal in data: describes symptoms in non-category language ("I'm just always tired", "I can't seem to get anything done").
Marketing implication: educate before selling — lead with symptom language.

### L2 — Pain-Aware
Buyer knows something is wrong but hasn't framed it as a solvable problem.
Signal in data: describes frustration without seeking solutions ("Every day feels the same", "I don't know what's wrong with me").
Marketing implication: validate the pain before introducing the category.

### L3 — Solution-Aware
Buyer knows solutions exist but hasn't found the right one.
Signal in data: compares approaches, reads reviews, asks "what works" ("I've tried apps but nothing sticks", "Which method actually works?").
Marketing implication: differentiate from generic solutions — show why yours is different.

### L4 — Product-Aware
Buyer has tried competitors. Has specific opinions.
Signal in data: names specific products, identifies what failed ("I bought [competitor] and it didn't work because...", "The problem with most ebooks is...").
Marketing implication: explicitly address competitor failure points — this is where Gap Résultat (G6) maps directly.

### L5 — Most Aware
Buyer is ready to purchase and just needs the right trigger.
Signal in data: asks about price, guarantee, delivery ("Does it include templates?", "Is there a money-back guarantee?", "How quickly can I see results?").
Marketing implication: price clearly, guarantee prominently, reduce friction.

## 5 Purchase Emotions (Detection Guide)

### FEAR_MISSING_OUT
Language signals: "everyone else seems to", "I'm getting left behind", "by the time I figure this out", "I'm watching others succeed while I..."
Common in: career, productivity, finance niches.

### SHAME
Language signals: "I should have figured this out by now", "I'm the only one who can't", "I'm embarrassed to admit", "I feel stupid for..."
Common in: personal finance, health, relationship niches. Handle carefully — never amplify, validate then redirect.

### HOPE
Language signals: "I just need the right system", "I know I can do this if", "I believe if someone could just show me", "this time I'm going to..."
Common in: self-improvement, productivity, business niches.

### FRUSTRATION
Language signals: "I've tried everything", "nothing works for me", "I'm sick of", "why is this so hard when it looks easy for others"
Common in: weight loss, finance, productivity niches.

### ASPIRATION
Language signals: "I want to be the kind of person who", "in 5 years I see myself", "people like that are", "I've always wanted to..."
Common in: business, lifestyle, fitness niches.

## Primary Source Usage Rules

| Data Need | Correct Source | Prohibited Substitution |
|-----------|---------------|------------------------|
| Life situation / trigger | SOURCE A (Reddit posts) | Do not use TikTok for this |
| Emotional expressions | SOURCE C (TikTok comments) | Do not use reviews for emotions |
| Failed purchase experience | SOURCE B (Amazon 1★ reviews) | Do not use Reddit for this |
| Influence discovery behavior | SOURCE C (TikTok comments) | Do not use Reddit for this |

Rationale: Each source has distinct authenticity for specific data types. Mixing sources degrades persona accuracy and reduces operational usefulness for Skill 05.

## Verbatim Standards

A valid verbatim in Skill 04:
- Is quoted exactly as written (typos included)
- Has a verifiable URL
- Has a recorded date
- Is not paraphrased or "cleaned up"

An invalid verbatim (skill failure — do not deliver):
- "Something like: the user said they were frustrated" → paraphrase
- A quote without URL → unverifiable
- A quote assigned to wrong source → data integrity failure
